What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the entire amount of greenhouse gases produced by human actions.

How Does Carbon Affect Climate Change?


Carbon dioxide absorbs heat from the sun and the Earth's surface before releasing it into the atmosphere. High quantities of greenhouse gases, notably carbon dioxide, threaten to raise the planet's average surface temperature to unacceptable levels and create a slew of life-threatening consequences. 

Since the middle of the 18th century, atmospheric carbon dioxide levels have increased by more than 40%, and climatologists estimate that present levels are the highest in 14 million years. As carbon dioxide levels rise, fuelling even higher temperature rises, the cumulative repercussions — such as increased ocean acidification, rising sea levels, more frequent and powerful storms, mass species extinctions, food scarcity, and increased economic hardship — will become more apparent.
